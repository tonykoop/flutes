# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

A finished Native American style wooden flute photographed as a real physical object, laid at a gentle three-quarter angle on a clean neutral seamless studio backdrop. The flute is a slender end-blown wooden tube, hand-turned to a smooth round cross-section, with the unmistakable signature feature resting on top: a removable carved wooden bird/fetish block held down by a leather lacing thong, seated in a shallow sunken nest where the upper slow-air chamber meets the long playing chamber. A row of six finger holes runs along the body toward the foot end, and the small rectangular true sound hole sits just under the leading edge of the block where the air is directed across it. The wood shows warm natural grain and a satin oil finish — figure consistent with cedar, walnut, or maple — with crisp chamfered hole edges and a flush block-to-body fit. Realistic scale cues read it as an arm-length handheld flute. Soft directional studio light, ~50mm lens, shallow but controlled depth of field, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

A maker's bench scene: one completed Native American style wooden flute lying on a worn workshop bench among fine cedar shavings and a plunge router fixture used to cut the slow-air-chamber and true-sound-hole geometry, with two or three more flutes in different wood species racked softly out of focus behind it. Warm natural window light rakes across the grain, the laced bird/fetish block catching a highlight on its carved top. Environmental storytelling of a multi-year hand-build practice, shallow depth of field, concept visualization only, photorealistic, no text labels.

## Detail Prompt

Macro close-up of the signature voicing mechanism: the carved wooden bird/fetish block lifted slightly off its sunken nest to reveal the precisely carved channel on its underside that directs air across the true sound hole, with the small rectangular sound hole and the flue gap visible on the flute body below it, leather lacing loosened to one side. Raking light grazes the carved channel and the chamfered edge of the sound hole, emphasizing tool-cut wood surfaces, fine grain, and the satin finish. High detail, shallow depth of field, no measurements, photorealistic.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks; no dimension callouts or measurement lines; no impossible mechanisms, no integrated recorder-style fipple (the block must be external and removable); anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
