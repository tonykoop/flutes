# What Wondrous Love Is This

One of the great Appalachian hymns. The lyrics first appear in print in
Stith Mead's 1811 *General Selection of the Newest and Most Admired
Hymns and Spiritual Songs Now in Use* — solidly public domain. The
melody (sometimes called "Wondrous Love" or attributed to the tune
"Captain Kidd" in shape-note tradition) was first paired with these
lyrics in William Walker's *Southern Harmony* (1835). Also unambiguously
PD.

## Provenance

- **Lyrics:** Stith Mead, 1811 — public domain.
- **Melody:** trad. Appalachian shape-note (often credited as the tune
  "Captain Kidd"), paired with the lyrics in William Walker's *Southern
  Harmony*, 1835 — public domain.
- **This canonical ABC arrangement:** Tony Koop / Heifer Zephyr, 2026,
  MIT-licensed.

## Why this arrangement uses A minor pentatonic

The original is **dorian** (raised 6th relative to natural minor),
which is what gives it that distinctive "ancient" sound. A strict
6-hole NAF can't play the raised 6th without a half-hole bend, so this
catalog entry simplifies to A minor pentatonic. The dorian colour
isn't fully preserved, but the *contour* — the lilting 3/4 phrases,
the climb to the upper tonic, the falling cadence — survives intact.

For instruments that *can* play the dorian 6th (kena with half-hole,
duduk, violin, harp), the dorian version is preferable. Add an
F#-natural at the relevant moments and re-deposit; v0.2 of this skill
should ship a dorian variant alongside the pentatonic one.

## Form

Three 4-bar phrases over a 14-measure verse. A-A-B-A-ish with the
final phrase resolving to the tonic. The 3/4 lilt is essential — don't
flatten it into 4/4.

## Per-instrument transposition guide

| Instrument | Target key | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| NAF (A-tuned, 6-hole) | A minor pent | Canonical fit; play as written. |
| NAF (G-tuned) | G minor pent | Transpose down a step. |
| Kena (G min pent) | G dorian | Use the half-hole F# for the true modal flavour. |
| Drone flute (A-anchored) | A | Drone reinforces the tonic. |
| Duduk (A min) | A dorian | Use the duduk's natural F# fingering. |
| Floor harp | A minor / dorian | Lever F# for the dorian version. |

## Practice tip

The tune's character is in the **3/4 lilt** and the **tonic-octave
climb**. Count "ONE-two-three" aloud while you play; the downbeat is
the spine. The phrase that reaches up to A5 ("to lay aside His crown
for my soul") is the emotional centre — give the climb a slight swell.

If you're playing this on a NAF and it feels too cheery in pentatonic
mode, slow it down further (Q:1/4=72) and lean on the long notes —
that recovers some of the dorian gravity.
