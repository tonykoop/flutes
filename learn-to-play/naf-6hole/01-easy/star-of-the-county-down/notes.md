# Star of the County Down

A traditional Irish melody with deep roots in the British Isles modal
ballad family — the same melodic backbone as "Dives and Lazarus,"
"Kingsfold," and "The Murder of Maria Marten." Variants of the tune
appear in George Petrie's *Ancient Music of Ireland* (1855) and in
Vaughan Williams's early-1900s collections. The melody is pre-1855 and
unambiguously public domain.

The lyrics most singers know — "Near Banbridge town in the County
Down…" — are by the Donegal-born poet Cathal MacGarvey (c. 1900) and
are also PD in the United States by virtue of pre-1929 publication.

## Provenance

- **Melody:** trad. Irish, "Dives and Lazarus" family, pre-1855 —
  public domain.
- **Lyrics:** Cathal MacGarvey, c. 1900 — public domain (US).
- **This canonical ABC arrangement:** Tony Koop / Heifer Zephyr, 2026,
  MIT-licensed.

## Why this arrangement uses A minor pentatonic

The original is **A dorian** — minor with a raised 6th (F#), which is
exactly what gives the tune its distinctive bittersweet lift. A strict
6-hole A-tuned NAF can't play the F# cleanly, so this catalog entry
simplifies to A minor pentatonic: the F# moments are smoothed into the
neighbouring G or E. The contour, the AABB structure, the climb to
the upper tonic — all preserved.

For instruments that *can* hit the dorian 6th (kena half-hole, duduk,
violin, floor harp with the F-lever set), the dorian version is
worth chasing. v0.2 of the skill should ship a dorian variant in
parallel.

## Form

AABB, eight bars per section, four bars per phrase. The B section
("From Bantry Bay up to Derry Quay…") is the climb — it reaches up to
A5 and is the emotional centre. The A section sits lower and more
contained.

## Per-instrument transposition guide

| Instrument | Target key | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| NAF (A-tuned, 6-hole) | A minor pent | Canonical fit; play as written. |
| NAF (G-tuned) | G minor pent | Transpose down a step. |
| Kena (G min pent) | G dorian | Use the half-hole F# for the true modal flavour. |
| Drone flute (A-anchored) | A | Drone reinforces the tonic. |
| Duduk (A min) | A dorian | Use the duduk's natural F# fingering. |
| Acoustic violin | D dorian | Transpose up a 4th for first-position friendliness. |
| Floor harp | A dorian | Lever F# for the modal version. |

## Practice tip

The tune has a **walking pulse** — feel it as a steady ¼-note step,
not a swung lilt. The B section climb to A5 is where most beginners
rush; deliberately slow that bar by a hair so the high note has room
to bloom.

If you're playing this on a NAF, the missing F# isn't a defect —
lean into the pentatonic version. Slow the tempo to ♩=72 and let
the long cadence notes ring; the tune holds up beautifully without
the dorian colour.
