# Simple Gifts (arranged for A-NAF)

Joseph Brackett's 1848 Shaker dance song, made famous outside the
Shaker community by Aaron Copland's 1944 use of it in *Appalachian
Spring*. The 1848 melody is solidly public domain.

## What this arrangement does

The canonical version (in `catalog/public-domain/appalachian/simple-gifts/`)
is in F major and uses notes outside A minor pentatonic. This
arrangement **re-keys the melody to A minor pentatonic** (tonic A,
notes A C D E G) so it plays cleanly on the A-tuned NAF.

The tune's characteristic 5-2-1 cadence — "tis the gift to be **simple**" —
maps as g → d → A, preserving the falling shape that gives the line
its lilt.

This is in `02-intermediate` because the brisker tempo (♩=104) and the
syncopated phrase entries make it harder than the slower hymns and
ballads in `01-easy`.

## Provenance

- **Melody and lyrics:** Joseph Brackett, Shaker community at Alfred,
  Maine, 1848 — public domain.
- **This A-NAF arrangement:** Tony Koop / Heifer Zephyr, 2026,
  MIT-licensed.

## Form

AABA, 16 bars at L:1/8 in 4/4. Each phrase is 4 bars; the B section
introduces the higher register but doesn't push past A5.

## Practice tip

The trick to Simple Gifts is the **dance lift** — keep the eighth notes
crisp and the long notes resonant. Don't slur the eighths; tongue each
one ("tu-tu" or "du-du").

The repeated A4 cadences ("'tis a gift to be **free**") are the
breath-and-balance moments. Use them: take a quiet breath in the
silence after each `A4 A2 z2` and you'll have plenty of air for the
next phrase.

If the tempo feels tight at ♩=104, drop to ♩=88 and let the line
breathe. The dance is in the *contour*, not the speed.
