# Twinkle Twinkle Little Star (arranged for A-NAF)

The melody is from W. A. Mozart's variations on "Ah! vous dirai-je,
maman" (K. 265, 1781). The English nursery lyrics ("Twinkle, twinkle,
little star…") are by Jane Taylor, *Rhymes for the Nursery*, 1806.
Both melody and lyrics are public domain.

## What this arrangement does

The canonical version (in `catalog/public-domain/nursery/twinkle-twinkle/`)
is in C major and uses an F passing tone that doesn't fit A minor
pentatonic. This arrangement re-keys to A minor pent (tonic A, notes
A C D E G) and substitutes the F passing tones with G or E (nearest
neighbours).

## Provenance

- **Melody:** Mozart, K. 265, 1781 — public domain.
- **Lyrics:** Jane Taylor, 1806 — public domain.
- **This A-NAF arrangement:** Tony Koop / Heifer Zephyr, 2026,
  MIT-licensed.

## Form

ABCB, 24 bars (each line of the rhyme is 4 bars). The "How I wonder
what you are…" line repeats; the middle "Up above the world so high…"
section sits a step lower in pitch.

## Practice tip

This tune is beginner-familiar but it has a wider interval at the
opening (A → e is a perfect 5th) than any of the easy tunes. Practice
that single jump in isolation: A4 to E5, ten times, until the embouchure
shift feels automatic. Then string it together.

The middle section (`e2 e2 d2 d2 | c2 c2 A4`) is good practice for
controlled descending motion — keep each note evenly weighted; don't
let the line slump.
